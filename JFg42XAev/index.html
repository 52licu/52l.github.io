Another championship, and FMVP? Why is Faker still so fierce at the age of 28?

2024-07-08 · 5 min read
Faker League of Legends LOL

Early this morning, the " League of Legends " event of 　　the 2024 Saudi Arabia Esports World Cup officially came to an end. The T1 team defeated the TES team 3:1 to win the championship, and Faker won the FMVP.

If this was Faker who just debuted in 2013, we might think it was normal, but 10 years later, why is the 28-year-old Faker still able to stand firm in the e-sports field and become the "evergreen" of "League of Legends"?

We all know that most e-sports players are between 17 and 22 years old, which is scientifically considered to be the peak of intelligence and reaction. After this age, the level of each aspect begins to decline, so many e-sports players choose to retire at the age of 24.

　　Like other professional players, Faker joined the e-sports industry at the age of 17, but he reached the peak as soon as he debuted. In his first year with the SKT team (renamed T1 in 2019), he single-killed South Korea's No. 1 mid laner An Zhangmen and South Korea's passerby Wang Yuelun with his outstanding strength, and won the S3 World Championship that year. The name of the newcomer Faker gradually appeared in everyone's vision.

　　In the following years, Faker's STK team also overcame all difficulties and won the world championship in S5 and S6. Faker's reputation became more and more famous, so much so that for a long time Faker became a synonym, and almost all players were proud to kill Faker alone.

　　However, all this changed after the defeat in the S7 World Championship. Many players left the team, and SKT began to decline. Faker, who was getting older, became a substitute for a while. Words like "Faker is old" and "Faker should retire" appeared one after another. However, Faker did not give up and continued to march towards his fourth championship.

　　In the S13 World Championship, Faker broke the doubts of the players and proved with his actions that "Lian Po is old, can he still fight?" Faker led the new team members to the top again after 7 years, and T1 added another star on the uniform. This also made Faker an insurmountable mountain in the history of "League of Legends" e-sports.

　　Looking back at Faker's life, he has fallen from the peak to the bottom, and then climbed back to the top. If this were put in a novel, it would definitely be a cool male protagonist plot. But Faker's success can never be summed up by the word "talent" alone.

　　For those who have won the S-League world championship, they can earn a lot of money just by live streaming. But with his love for League of Legends, Faker has been on the road of e-sports year after year. In order to make up for the gap caused by his age, Faker only has continuous training in his eyes.

Previously, a blogger had posted Faker's daily schedule, which showed that Faker's daily activities included: sleeping, eating, training, playing solo, free activities, and training outside of games. Among them, the game time took up 12 hours, and training and playing solo each took up 6 hours. To borrow Faker's words: I never stopped working hard, I never chose to give up, so I believe I can definitely get back to the top.

　　As for when Faker will retire, Faker has previously stated that he wants to continue staying with the T1 team, and emphasized that as a professional player, he has been working hard to learn and grow, and he will continue to seize this rare opportunity.

　　Although Faker is in LCK, his professional attitude and professionalism are worth learning for our LPL professional players. I hope that one day the LPL region can have a benchmark figure like Faker, leading the region to greater glory and achievement.
